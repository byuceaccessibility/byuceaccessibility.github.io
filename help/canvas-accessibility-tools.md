# Web Accessibility Resource Hub

This guide provides the most effective, free tools for ensuring your digital content is accessible to everyone.

---

## 🛠️ Essential Browsers
For the best testing experience, use these browsers:
* [**Google Chrome**](https://www.google.com/chrome/) – The industry standard for accessibility testing.
* [**Mozilla Firefox**](https://www.mozilla.org/en-US/firefox/new) – A great secondary browser for cross-checking.

---

## 🎨 Color & Contrast
Ensure your text is readable for users with low vision or color blindness.
* [**WebAIM Contrast Checker**](https://webaim.org/resources/contrastchecker) – A simple site to check if your text and background colors pass accessibility standards.
* [**Accessible Colors**](https://accessible-colors.com/) – If your colors fail, this tool automatically finds the closest colors that *do* pass.
* [**Stark Extension**](https://chrome.google.com/webstore/detail/stark/fkfaapnmfippddbeemjjbclenphooipm) – A browser extension that simulates 9 types of color blindness and vision impairments.

---

## 🔍 Automated Page Checkers
Get an instant report on accessibility errors like missing alt-text or broken heading structures.
* [**WAVE (Web Accessibility Evaluation Tool)**](https://wave.webaim.org/extension/) – One of the most popular extensions. It provides visual icons directly on your page to show you exactly what needs fixing.
* [**Silktide Simulator**](https://chrome.google.com/webstore/detail/silktide-website-accessib/okcpiimdfkpkjcbihbmhppldhiebhhaf) – Experience your site through the lens of a user with dyslexia, cataracts, or blindness.

---

## 🎧 Screen Readers & Reading Aids
Hear how your content sounds to users who are blind or have learning disabilities.
* [**NVDA (Windows)**](https://www.nvaccess.org/download) – A free, world-class screen reader.
* [**VoiceOver (Mac/iOS)**](https://www.apple.com/accessibility/vision/) – Already built into your Apple devices; no installation required.
* [**Speechify**](https://speechify.com/) – A text-to-speech tool that reads content aloud, helping users with ADHD or dyslexia.

---

## 📄 PDF & Document Accessibility
* [**PAC (PDF Accessibility Checker)**](https://pac.pdf-accessibility.org/en) – The global standard for checking if a PDF is truly accessible to screen readers.
* [**Microsoft/Adobe Built-in Checkers**](https://support.microsoft.com/en-us/office/improve-accessibility-with-the-accessibility-checker-a16f6de0-2f39-4a27-8d74-74738403d57d) – Don't forget to use the "Check Accessibility" button already found inside Word, PowerPoint, and Acrobat.

---

## 🌐 Quick Learning Guides
* [**W3C: How People with Disabilities Use the Web**](https://www.w3.org/WAI/people-use-web/) – Real-life stories and videos about different user needs.
* [**A11y Project Checklist**](https://www.a11yproject.com/checklist/) – A beginner-friendly checklist for any web project.
